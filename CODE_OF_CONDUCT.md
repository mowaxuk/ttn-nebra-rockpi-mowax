# Code of Conduct

Everyone who shows up here — whether you're reporting a bug, submitting a fix, or just asking a question — deserves to be treated with patience and respect.

---

## What we ask for

- **Patience with newcomers.** LoRaWAN, Linux GPIO, and embedded systems each have steep learning curves. Questions that seem basic often represent hours of prior effort.
- **Document failures, not just successes.** A note saying "I tried X and it didn't work because Y" is often more valuable than a polished solution.
- **Constructive feedback.** If someone's approach is wrong, explain why and what works better.
- **Credit where it's due.** If someone else's debugging work helped you, say so.

---

## What we won't tolerate

- Dismissing questions as obvious
- "Just use a Raspberry Pi" or equivalent hardware-shaming
- Personal attacks, insults, or harassment of any kind
- Sharing someone's private information without their consent

---

## Scope

This applies to all project spaces: GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and commit comments.

---

## Enforcement

Violations may result in comment removal, issue closure, or banning from the project. To report a problem, use GitHub's private vulnerability reporting or contact the maintainer directly.
